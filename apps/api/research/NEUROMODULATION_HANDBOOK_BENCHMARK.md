## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Research Scope

This benchmark study analyzed 40+ publicly available neuromodulation clinical documents across eight categories to identify best practices for handbook/manual/guide design. The analysis focused on structural patterns, safety content organization, evidence-grading approaches, patient/clinician-facing formatting, and operational workflows.

### Key Findings at a Glance

| Dimension | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **Average handbook length** | 40-110 pages (clinical guidelines); 2-4 pages (SOPs); 1-2 pages (checklists) |
| **Most common sections** | Safety/contraindications (100%), indications (95%), protocol parameters (90%), patient information (85%) |
| **Evidence grading** | GRADE system dominates in academic contexts; NICE uses custom evidence tables; FDA uses regulatory classification |
| **Safety approach** | Tiered risk classification (absolute contraindications → considerations → precautions) is standard |
| **Target audience split** | Separate documents for clinicians vs. patients; dual-audience documents use color-coding or appendix separation |
| **Consent complexity** | 12-18 specific risk items; 8-10 metal screening checkboxes; 4-6 patient acknowledgments |

## 6. EVIDENCE-GRADING PATTERN BENCHMARKS

### 6.1 GRADE System in Neuromodulation

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Source URL** | https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12182506/ (Migraine example) |
| **Document type** | Systematic review with GRADE |
| **Target audience** | Researchers, guideline developers |

#### GRADE Application Pattern

**Downgrading factors:**
- Risk of bias (allocation concealment, blinding)
- Inconsistency (unexplained heterogeneity)
- Indirectness (different populations/interventions)
- Imprecision (wide confidence intervals)
- Publication bias

**GRADE evidence levels observed:**

| Level | Certainty | Typical Use |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| High | Very certain | Major depression rTMS (high-frequency) |
| Moderate | Moderately certain | tDCS for depression |
| Low | Limited certainty | ONS for migraine |
| Very Low | Very uncertain | Accelerated TMS protocols |

#### Evidence Summary Table Format

| Outcome | Studies (n) | Participants | Effect | Certainty |
|---------|-------------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| >=50% responder rate | 5 | 427 active / 91 placebo | 36.3% vs 13.2% | Very Low |
| Treatment-emergent AEs | 6 | 321 active / 74 placebo | 53% vs 45.9% | Very Low |

## 8. CLINICAL SOP TEMPLATE BENCHMARKS

### 8.1 tDCS Standard Operating Procedures (Comprehensive)

Based on PMC Clinical Practice Guidelines for tDCS in Psychiatry:

#### Phase 1: Pre-Session (Recruitment)
- Indication and precaution ascertainment
- Detailed procedure explanation (video recommended)
- 15-item screening questionnaire
- Written informed consent

#### Phase 2: Preparation
- Material check (7 items listed)
- Device inspection
- Electrode preparation (saline quantity specified)
- Target localization (10-20 system, BeamF3, or MRI)

#### Phase 3: Administration (17 Steps)
1. Seat subject comfortably
2. Inspect scalp for lesions
3. Localize target regions
4. Mark scalp locations
5. Part hair, apply EEG paste if needed
6. Switch on device BEFORE electrode placement
7. Verify electrode orientation (smooth surface contact)
8. Sponge preparation (6 ml saline per side)
9. Place electrodes with minimum 7 cm distance
10. Check headband comfort
11. Set electrical parameters (intensity, duration, ramp)
12. Initiate treatment, monitor impedance (<10-15 kW)
13. Check sensations, reassure
14. Troubleshoot impedance issues (4-step protocol)
15. Post-session: remove electrodes, THEN switch off
16. Clean electrodes with tissue
17. Clean sponges, dry completely

#### Phase 4: Post-Session
1. Inspect skin for irritation/damage
2. Document session details in record sheet
3. Enquire about side effects, record in side-effect sheet
4. Inform about next session timing
5. Check power status, charge if needed

#### Phase 5: Ongoing Precautions
- Regular head-bath, no oiling
- Skin inspection before and after every session
- Subject must be relaxed, comfortable, awake
- No intensive cognitive effort during session
- Device on/off safety sequence
- Uncoil wires before stimulation

## 10. TOP 10 CROSS-CUTTING RECOMMENDATIONS FOR DEEPSYNAPS

### Recommendation 1: Adopt a Tiered Safety Framework

**Finding:** All best-practice documents use a 3-tier safety classification:
- **Absolute Contraindications** (proceed = stop)
- **Considerations/Risk Factors** (proceed = discuss with physician)
- **Precautions** (proceed = monitor)

**Recommendation:** Implement this 3-tier structure consistently across all DeepSynaps handbooks. Each neuromodality (TMS, tDCS, neurofeedback) should have its own tiered safety matrix in the handbook's safety chapter.

---

### Recommendation 2: Create Separate but Linked Clinician and Patient Documents

**Finding:** Leading guidelines (Danish Psychiatric Society, NICE) produce separate documents for clinicians (technical) and patients (plain language), with cross-references.

**Recommendation:** Design the DeepSynaps handbook system with:
- **Clinician Handbook** (~100 pages): protocols, evidence, dosing, troubleshooting
- **Patient Guide** (~10-15 pages): plain-language explanations, preparation instructions, what to expect
- **Quick Reference Card** (~2 pages): checklist format for session use

---

### Recommendation 3: Standardize Chapter Templates Across Modalities

**Finding:** The Neurofeedback Protocol Atlas uses a uniform 3-section chapter format (Principles & Targets → Applications & Indications → Implementation & Practice) that enables rapid lookup and comparison.

**Recommendation:** Apply this template consistently:
- **Section 1: Mechanism & Targets** — physiology, electrode/coil placement, parameters
- **Section 2: Indications & Evidence** — conditions, GRADE ratings, key studies
- **Section 3: Protocol & Safety** — step-by-step procedures, guardrails, troubleshooting

---

### Recommendation 4: Integrate Screening Checklists Directly into Handbooks

**Finding:** Best-practice safety checklists (Rossi 2021, Auckland, APNA) are designed as standalone operational tools with checkboxes, not narrative text.

**Recommendation:** Include printable/downloadable checklists in every handbook:
- Pre-session safety screen (15 items for tDCS, 8-10 absolute + 6-8 considerations for TMS)
- Adverse effects monitoring log (19 items for tDCS)
- Session documentation template (5 essential elements)

---

### Recommendation 5: Use Structured Evidence Summary Tables

**Finding:** NICE overviews use standardized evidence tables with study details, critical appraisal, efficacy outcomes, and safety outcomes in columns. GRADE uses summary-of-findings tables.

**Recommendation:** Include evidence summary tables for each indication:
| Study | Design | n= | Intervention | Comparator | Primary Outcome | Result | Certainty |

Use GRADE terminology (High/Moderate/Low/Very Low) for all evidence statements.

---

### Recommendation 6: Include Complete SOPs with Quantified Parameters

**Finding:** The PMC tDCS guidelines specify exact quantities ("6 ml saline per sponge"), thresholds ("impedance below 10-15 kW"), and distances ("minimum 7 cm between electrodes").

**Recommendation:** All protocol SOPs should include:
- Exact numerical parameters (not "small amount" but "6 ml")
- Pass/fail thresholds for all measurements
- Step numbering for traceability
- Troubleshooting decision trees

---

### Recommendation 7: Build in Training Competency Requirements

**Finding:** Danish guidelines specify "minimum training requirements and continuing education for TMS staff." BCIA neurofeedback certification requires 25 contact hours, 100 client sessions, 10 case studies.

**Recommendation:** Include a Training & Competency chapter:
- Minimum training hours per modality
- Supervision requirements (direct vs. phone availability)
- Competency assessment criteria
- Continuing education recommendations
- Certification requirements where applicable

---

### Recommendation 8: Design for Session-by-Session Documentation

**Finding:** Neurofeedback session documentation requires 5 elements (session ID, protocol parameters, objective data, clinical observations, client self-report). TMS consent requires ongoing side effect monitoring.

**Recommendation:** Include documentation templates in handbooks:
- Session note template with all required fields
- Longitudinal outcome tracking table
- Protocol change log with rationale
- Adverse event reporting form

---

### Recommendation 9: Create Explicit Patient Preparation Instructions

**Finding:** The Gleneagles tDCS leaflet includes practical instructions: "Have hair washing before the day of examination. Do not put on any hair styling gel, hair wax, hair spray."

**Recommendation:** Every patient-facing document should include:
- Pre-session preparation checklist (hair, clothing, jewelry, eating)
- What to bring (hearing protection for TMS, etc.)
- Arrival time and session duration
- Post-session activity restrictions (if any)
- Contact information for questions

---

### Recommendation 10: Plan for Version Control and Evidence Updates

**Finding:** Danish guidelines state: "The field of TMS is rapidly evolving, which means some data and recommendations will eventually become outdated." NICE has formal surveillance processes.

**Recommendation:** Build version control into the handbook system:
- Version number and date on every page
- Evidence review schedule (annual for rapidly evolving areas)
- Change log tracking updates
- "Date of evidence" notation per protocol
- Digital-first format enabling push updates
