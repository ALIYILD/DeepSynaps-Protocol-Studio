# Digital Phenotyping Evidence Matrix

## Executive Summary

This matrix documents evidence for 18 digital phenotyping signal types used in passive behavioural monitoring. All signals are framed as exploratory decision-support cues requiring clinician review.

---

## 1. Sleep Timing

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Signal** | Bedtime / wake time regularity |
| **Evidence** | B — Multiple RCTs and longitudinal studies |
| **Effect size** | Sleep irregularity r=0.40 with MADRS (Cho et al. 2023) |
| **Sample** | 2,000+ participants across 5 studies |
| **Clinical use** | Clinical adjunct — supports sleep assessment |
| **Research-only** | No — well-validated |
| **Confounders** | Shift work, travel, illness, medication |
| **Safe wording** | "Sleep timing patterns may support clinical sleep assessment. Irregular sleep is associated with mood symptoms in research studies. Requires clinical confirmation." |
| **Sensors** | Accelerometer, screen on/off, manual input |
| **Integration** | Links to CBT-I sleep diary, Video Analyzer sleep tasks |

## 2. Sleep Duration

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Signal** | Total sleep time per night |
| **Evidence** | A — Meta-analyses confirm association |
| **Effect size** | Short sleep (<6h) OR=1.31 for depression (Zhai et al. 2015 meta-analysis) |
| **Sample** | 100,000+ participants (meta-analytic) |
| **Clinical use** | Routine clinical assessment |
| **Research-only** | No — well-established |
| **Confounders** | Age, medication, caffeine, sleep disorders |
| **Safe wording** | "Sleep duration is a well-established health indicator. Short or long sleep may support clinical review of sleep hygiene and mood." |
| **Sensors** | Accelerometer, wearable, manual diary |
| **Integration** | Biomarkers sleep tab, Assessments sleep scales |
