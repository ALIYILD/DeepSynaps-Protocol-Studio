## 1.1 CYP2D6

### 1.1.1 Gene Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Gene** | CYP2D6 (Cytochrome P450 Family 2 Subfamily D Member 6) |
| **Location** | 22q13.2 |
| **Function** | Oxidative metabolism of ~25% of clinically used drugs |
| **Polymorphism** | Highly polymorphic; >100 allelic variants cataloged |
| **Inheritance** | Autosomal codominant with gene duplication/deletion (CNV) |
| **Protein** | CYP2D6 enzyme; accounts for ~2% of hepatic CYP content |
| **Substrates** | Primarily basic lipophilic amines |
| **Inducers** | Few (not significantly inducible) |
| **Inhibitors** | Fluoxetine, paroxetine, bupropion, quinidine (potent) |

### 1.1.5 Clinical Consequences by Phenotype

| Phenotype | Enzyme Activity | Drug Exposure | Clinical Risk | Monitoring Strategy |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **UM** | 200-400% of normal | 30-70% of normal | Therapeutic failure; toxicity from active metabolites | Consider alternative agent; monitor symptoms closely |
| **EM** | 100% (reference) | Normal | Standard | Standard monitoring |
| **IM** | 50-100% of normal | 120-200% of normal | Increased side effects; partial response | Start low, go slow; consider dose reduction |
| **PM** | 0-10% of normal | 200-500% of normal | Significant ADR risk; cardiotoxicity (TCAs) | Avoid or use 25-50% dose; strong alternative preference |

BLACK BOX WARNING: SERIOUS DERMATOLOGIC REACTIONS

Serious and sometimes fatal dermatologic reactions, including 
Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN) and Stevens-Johnson Syndrome 
(SJS), have been reported with carbamazepine treatment. 

The risk of SJS/TEN is significantly increased in patients 
carrying the HLA-B*1502 allele, found predominantly in 
patients of Asian ancestry, including Han Chinese, Thai, 
Indian, and Filipino populations.

FDA RECOMMENDATION:
- Screen for HLA-B*1502 in patients of Asian ancestry BEFORE 
  initiating carbamazepine
- Do NOT use carbamazepine in HLA-B*1502 positive patients 
  unless benefits clearly outweigh risks
- Consider alternative anticonvulsants in HLA-B*1502 carriers
```

### 5.3.1 Pre-Prescribing Pharmacogenomic Testing Algorithm

```

|   |

|   |

|   |   --> ANK3 + CACNA1C for lithium response prediction (research)
|   |
|   +-- Anti-dementia
|       --> CYP2D6 for donepezil

|   |

### 5.4.2 Phenocopying Management

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| Patient on fluoxetine; genotype unknown | Assume phenotypic PM for any new CYP2D6 drug; washout 4-6 weeks for accurate genotyping |
| Patient on fluoxetine; genotype known as EM/UM | Treat as phenotypic PM; fluoxetine masks true genotype |
| Switching from fluoxetine to paroxetine | No washout needed (both potent CYP2D6 inhibitors) |
| Switching from fluoxetine to sertraline | Washout 4-6 weeks for CYP2D6 recovery; sertraline is weak inhibitor |
| Adding CYP2D6 substrate to fluoxetine | Reduce substrate dose 50% (especially aripiprazole, risperidone, atomoxetine) |

## S1.1 CYP2D6 Ultra-Rapid Metabolizer (UM) Clinical Management

### Definition and Detection

| Characteristic | Detail |
|---------------|--------|
| **Activity Score** | Greater than 2.0 (typically 2.0-3.0, rarely higher) |
| **Molecular Basis** | Duplication, triplication, or quadruplication of fully functional alleles (*1xN, *2xN where N=2, 3, 4) |
| **Copy Number** | 3-13 copies reported; clinical significance plateaus above 3 |
| **Global Prevalence** | 5-10% overall; 1-2% for triplication |
| **Ethnic Variation** | Highest in North African, Middle Eastern, and Ethiopian populations (up to 30%); lowest in East Asian populations (<1%) |
| **Enzyme Activity** | 200-400% of wild-type; can reach 500%+ with multiple copies |

# SUPPLEMENT 6: METHODOLOGY AND EVIDENCE GRADING

## S6.1 Evidence Grading Criteria

### Grade A: Strong Evidence

| Criterion | Requirement | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Replication | 2+ independent RCTs or meta-analysis with >1000 subjects | CYP2D6-atomoxetine (CPIC 1A) |
| Effect size | Clinically meaningful (OR >2 or SMD >0.5 for continuous) | CYP2D6-TCA toxicity (3-5x AUC) |
| Biological plausibility | Clear mechanism; pharmacokinetic validation | CYP2D6 enzyme activity directly measured |
| Clinical utility | Actionable dosing recommendation with safety improvement | HLA-B*15:02 carbamazepine avoidance |
| Guideline support | CPIC Level 1A or 1B; FDA actionable label | CYP2D6-nortriptyline (CPIC 1A) |

### Grade B: Moderate Evidence

| Criterion | Requirement | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Replication | 2+ studies with generally consistent results | CYP2D6-fluoxetine (2-5x AUC) |
| Effect size | Moderate (OR 1.5-2 or SMD 0.3-0.5) | CYP2C19-diazepam (3-5x AUC) |
| Biological plausibility | Reasonable mechanism; some PK validation | CYP2C19 enzyme activity correlation |
| Clinical utility | Dosing recommendation with probable benefit | CPIC Level 2A or 2B |
| Limitations | Some heterogeneity; smaller sample sizes | SLC6A4-SSRI response |

### Grade C: Limited Evidence

| Criterion | Requirement | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Replication | 1-2 studies; inconsistent results | HTR2A-antipsychotic weight gain |
| Effect size | Small-moderate; may not be clinically meaningful | OR 1.2-1.5 |
| Biological plausibility | Theoretical; limited validation | Pharmacodynamic predictions |
| Clinical utility | May inform but not guide treatment | Optional testing consideration |
| Limitations | Significant heterogeneity; small N | ANK3-lithium response |

### Grade D: Insufficient Evidence

| Criterion | Requirement | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Replication | Unreplicated or contradictory | CYP1A2-olanzapine precision dosing |
| Effect size | Small or unmeasured | Subjective reports |
| Biological plausibility | Unclear or multiple competing hypotheses | Complex gene-gene interactions |
| Clinical utility | Not actionable | Research interest only |
| Limitations | Case reports; preclinical only | Novel variants without validation |

## S6.2 Statistical Methods in Pharmacogenomics

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Odds Ratio (OR)** | Ratio of odds of outcome in exposed vs unexposed | OR >1: Increased risk/response; OR <1: Decreased |
| **Hazard Ratio (HR)** | Ratio of event rates over time | HR >1: Faster event occurrence |
| **Standardized Mean Difference (SMD)** | Mean difference / pooled SD | 0.2=small, 0.5=medium, 0.8=large effect |
| **Number Needed to Genotype (NNG)** | Number of genotype tests to prevent one adverse event | Lower NNG = more cost-effective testing |
| **Number Needed to Treat (NNT)** | Number of patients treated for one to benefit | Lower NNT = more effective intervention |
| **Positive Predictive Value (PPV)** | Probability of outcome given positive test | High PPV = reliable positive result |
| **Negative Predictive Value (NPV)** | Probability of no outcome given negative test | High NPV = reliable negative result |
| **I2 (heterogeneity)** | % variance due to between-study differences | I2 <25%: low; 25-50%: moderate; >50%: high |

# SUPPLEMENT 12: GLOSSARY OF TERMS

## Pharmacogenomics Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Allele** | Alternative form of a gene at a specific chromosomal location |
| **Activity score** | Numeric system (CPIC) quantifying enzyme activity based on genotype |
| **Biomarker** | Measurable biological indicator of normal or pathological process |
| **Copy number variation (CNV)** | Insertion or deletion of gene copies; critical for CYP2D6 |
| **Drug-drug interaction (DDI)** | Alteration of drug effect by concurrent medication |
| **DRESS syndrome** | Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms |
| **EM (Extensive Metabolizer)** | Normal enzyme activity; standard drug metabolism |
| **Epistasis** | Gene-gene interaction where one gene masks the effect of another |
| **Gain-of-function variant** | Genetic variant that increases normal protein activity |
| **Genome-wide association study (GWAS)** | Method to identify genetic variants associated with traits across genome |
| **Genotype** | Genetic makeup of an individual at a specific locus |
| **Haplotype** | Set of genetic variants inherited together on same chromosome |
| **Heterozygous** | Having two different alleles at a genetic locus |
| **Homozygous** | Having two identical alleles at a genetic locus |
| **Hypervariable region** | Chromosomal region with high mutation/variant rate |
| **IM (Intermediate Metabolizer)** | Reduced but not absent enzyme activity |
| **Inducer** | Substance that increases expression/activity of metabolic enzyme |
| **Inhibitor** | Substance that decreases activity of metabolic enzyme |
| **Linkage disequilibrium (LD)** | Non-random association of alleles at different loci |
| **Loss-of-function variant** | Genetic variant that reduces or eliminates protein function |
| **Metabolizer phenotype** | Classification of drug metabolism capacity based on genotype |
| **Missense variant** | Point mutation resulting in different amino acid |
| **Nonsense variant** | Point mutation resulting in premature stop codon |
| **Pharmacodynamics (PD)** | Study of drug effects on the body (receptor binding, etc.) |
| **Pharmacokinetics (PK)** | Study of drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion |
| **Phenocopy** | When environment/medication produces phenotype mimicking genotype |
| **PM (Poor Metabolizer)** | Minimal or absent enzyme activity |
| **Polymorphism** | Genetic variation occurring in >1% of population |
| **Prodrug** | Inactive compound metabolized to active form |
| **Single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP)** | Single base pair variation at a specific position |
| **Splice site variant** | Mutation affecting mRNA splicing; often loss-of-function |
| **Star allele nomenclature** | System for naming CYP450 alleles (*1, *2, *4, etc.) |
| **Synonymous variant** | Mutation not changing amino acid sequence (silent) |
| **Therapeutic drug monitoring (tDM)** | Measuring drug levels to guide dosing |
| **TEN (Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis)** | Severe mucocutaneous reaction; often drug-induced |
| **UM (Ultrarapid Metabolizer)** | Increased enzyme activity due to gene duplication |
| **VNTR (Variable Number Tandem Repeat)** | Short DNA sequence repeated variable number of times |
| **Wild-type allele** | Most common/reference allele (*1 for CYP450 genes) |

-> Olanzapine: Increase 25-50% over 1-2 weeks

QUITTING SMOKING (CRITICAL - TOXICITY RISK):
-> Clozapine: REDUCE 50% before quit date
             Then adjust based on levels
             Target: 350-450 ng/mL
-> Olanzapine: REDUCE 30-50% before quit date
             Monitor for sedation/metabolic effects
-> Duloxetine: REDUCE 20-25%

NOTIFY PRESCRIBER IF:
- Patient expresses desire to quit
- Patient successfully quits
- Patient relapses to smoking
- Patient starts vaping/nicotine replacement

CYP1A2 INHIBITORS TO AVOID WITH CLOZAPINE:
- Fluvoxamine (MAJOR - avoid combination)
- Ciprofloxacin (reduce clozapine 50%)
- Oral contraceptives (may need dose adjustment)
