## 1. TMS (rTMS, iTBS, dTMS)

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | NNT |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) | **A** | HF-rTMS: Response 29-60%, Remission 18-23% vs sham | NNT=6 (response), NNT=8 (remission) |
| Treatment-Resistant Depression | **A** | iTBS non-inferior to 10Hz rTMS; connectivity-guided improves outcomes | NNT=10 |
| OCD | **A** | Bilateral DLPFC and mPFC/ACC targets effective; 45.2% response vs 17.8% sham | NNT varies by target |
| PTSD | **B** | dTMS + brief exposure showed promise; symptom provocation enhances response | Emerging |
| Smoking Cessation | **B** | FDA-cleared protocol: 28% continuous quit rate vs 11.7% sham; + symptom provocation | NNT=6 |
| Youth Depression (10-25y) | **A** | SMD=-0.93; RR=1.24 response, RR=1.63 remission; NNT=10 | NNT=10 |
| Anxious Depression | **B** | iTBS response 38-58%, remission 10-43%; both depression and anxiety improve | NNT=6-8 |

### Session Parameters (Standardized)

| Parameter | Standard HF-rTMS | iTBS | dTMS (MDD) | dTMS (OCD) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 10 Hz (HF) or 1 Hz (LF) | Theta burst (50Hz bursts at 5Hz) | 18-20 Hz | 20 Hz |
| Intensity | 120% RMT | 80-120% RMT | 120% MT | 120% MT |
| Pulses/session | 3000 (typical) | 600 pulses | ~1980 pulses/session | H7 coil |
| Sessions | 20-30 (4-6 weeks) | 20-30 (4-6 weeks) | 20-30 sessions | 20-29 sessions |
| Target | Left DLPFC | Left DLPFC | Bilateral PFC (H1 coil) | mPFC/ACC (H7 coil) |
| Duration/session | ~37 minutes | ~3.5 minutes | ~20 minutes | ~20 minutes |

### Outcome Measures
- **Primary:** HAM-D17/21, MADRS, PHQ-9, BDI-II
- **OCD:** Y-BOCS (response >35% reduction)
- **PTSD:** CAPS-5, PCL-5
- **Smoking:** Continuous quit rate, craving scales
- **Cognitive:** CANTAB, working memory assessments

### Response Rates from Meta-Analyses
- HF-rTMS for MDD: **29.3% response**, **18.6% remission** vs sham (10.4%/5%); pooled OR 3.3 (p<0.0001) [PMID: Meta-analysis, 29 RCTs, n=1371]
- iTBS for MDD: **38-60% response**, **10-43% remission** (varies by study design)
- Accelerated dTMS: **60.86% response** (MDD), **62.76% response** (OCD) at day 6-10
- Youth depression: **Remission RR 1.63** (95% CI 1.11-2.39), NNT=10

### Combination Approaches
- **TMS + Antidepressants:** Active TMS + antidepressants shows large effect vs sham TMS + antidepressants; continue antidepressants during TMS to avoid re-initiation
- **TMS + Psychotherapy:** Limited RCT data; mechanistic rationale for additive effects
- **Accelerated protocols:** Twice-daily HF + iTBS shows comparable outcomes to standard protocols in shorter timeframe

### Key References
- PMID: 2834609 (Symptom provocation meta-analysis, JAMA Psychiatry 2025)
- PMID: 12678628 (rTMS youth depression meta-analysis, 2024)
- PMC12243985 (iTBS depression/anxiety outcomes, Mayo Clinic)
- PMC12938041 (iTBS for comorbid anxiety systematic review)
- JAMA Network Open 2025 (Connectivity-guided iTMS vs rTMS RCT)
- PMC11524532 (dTMS OCD systematic review of meta-analyses)
- Nature 2024 (BRIGhTMIND RCT: connectivity-guided iTBS)

## 2. tDCS

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major Depression | **A** | Significant but modest effect; home-based feasible | WMD MADRS: -2.74; WMD HAM-D: -2.24 |
| Chronic Pain | **A** | Moderate analgesic effect; short-to-medium term | SMD=-0.43 (pain); SMD=-0.31 (depression) |
| Fibromyalgia Pain | **A** | Strong analgesic effect; pooled d=1.22 | d=1.22 (95% CI: 0.80-1.65) |
| Cognitive Enhancement (MDD) | **C** | Mixed results; verbal memory may improve | Small effects; inconsistent |
| Home-based tDCS (Pain) | **B** | Low-moderate certainty; safe and feasible | MD=-11.07 FIQ; MD=-10.15 BDI |

### Session Parameters

| Parameter | Depression | Chronic Pain | Fibromyalgia |
|---|---|---|---|
| Target | F3/F4 (DLPFC) | M1 or DLPFC | M1, DLPFC, SO |
| Intensity | 2 mA | 1.4-2 mA | 1.4-2 mA |
| Duration | 20-30 min | 20-30 min | 20-30 min |
| Sessions | 6-10+ | 5-15+ | 10-20 |
| Electrode | 35 cm2 | 35 cm2 | 35 cm2 |
| Home-based | Emerging (tele-supervised) | Feasible | Feasible |

### Outcome Measures
- Depression: MADRS, HAM-D, PHQ-9, BDI-II
- Pain: VAS, NRS, FIQ, PPT
- Cognitive: TMT, verbal memory tests

### Response Rates
- Home-based tDCS (depression): Statistically significant but may fall below MCID threshold
- Home-based tDCS (chronic pain): Clinically meaningful pain reduction; response rate ~60% at 6 months
- tDCS for fibromyalgia: Large analgesic effect (d=1.22) across 16 RCTs (n=813)

### Combination Approaches
- tDCS + cognitive training: improves white matter integrity in late-life depression (pilot study)
- tDCS + pharmacotherapy: may offer dual benefits for depression + anxiety
- tDCS + exercise: emerging multimodal approach (limited evidence)

### Key References
- PMC12485468 (tDCS pain umbrella review, 2025)
- Nature Scientific Reports 2025 (Home-based tDCS depression meta-analysis)
- PMC11590159 (Home-based tDCS chronic pain meta-analysis)
- PMC10203058 (tDCS fibromyalgia meta-analysis)

## 3. tACS

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alzheimer's/MCI (gamma 40Hz) | **B** | Improves cognition; combined with sound may enhance durability | ADAS-Cog improvements |
| Tinnitus | **C** | Mixed vs tDCS; alpha-tACS showed limited effects vs tDCS | Inconclusive |
| Cognitive Enhancement (MCI) | **B** | Gamma tACS > tDCS on Stroop-color; gamma > sham on TMT-B | p=0.044 vs tDCS |

### Session Parameters

| Parameter | Alzheimer's/MCI | Tinnitus | Cognitive Enhancement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 40 Hz (gamma) | Alpha (individualized) | Gamma (40 Hz) |
| Intensity | 2 mA | 1-2 mA | 1-2 mA |
| Duration | 20-60 min | 20-40 min | 20-40 min |
| Sessions | 15-40 | 5-10 | 10-20 |
| Target | DLPFC, temporal lobe | Auditory cortex | DLPFC |

### Key Findings
- tACS + sound stimulation for AD: First proposed multimodal approach; may produce greater/longer-lasting cognitive improvement than tACS alone
- 40Hz tACS targets gamma oscillation deficits in AD
- Combined with fMRI outcomes: changes in DMN connectivity correlate with cognitive improvements

### Key References
- PMC11700759 (tDCS/tACS tinnitus perceptual effects)
- PMC9869764 (tACS + sound stimulation AD trial)
- Clinical Psychopharmacology and Neuroscience 2026 (tACS vs tDCS head-to-head review)

## 4. tRNS

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major Depression | **B** | No significant difference vs sham in one RCT (n=130); safe up to 40 sessions | Null (primary outcome) |
| Fibromyalgia | **C** | Hypothesized to have stronger effects than tDCS due to broader frequency spectrum | Theoretical |
| Learning/Cognitive Enhancement | **C** | Greater cortical excitability than tDCS; may limit homeostatic responses | M1 excitability studies |

### Session Parameters

| Parameter | Depression | Cognitive Enhancement |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 0.1-640 Hz (broadband) | 0.1-640 Hz |
| Intensity | 1-2 mA (up to 3 mA with offset) | 1 mA |
| Duration | 20-40 min | 10-20 min |
| Sessions | Up to 40 (8 weeks) | Single or multiple |
| Target | DLPFC | Task-dependent |

### Key Findings
- tRNS may produce greater cortical excitability than tDCS (Moliadze et al., 2014)
- tRNS with DC offset may be more effective than without offset (Ho et al., 2015)
- Safe and well-tolerated; AEs: erythema, paresthesia, fatigue, dizziness (generally mild)
- Blinding may be challenging due to increased paresthesia

### Key References
- PMC7171931 (tRNS for depression RCT)
- Moliadze et al. 2014 (tRNS vs tDCS cortical excitability)
- Ho et al. 2015 (tRNS with DC offset)

## 5. taVNS (Transcutaneous Auricular Vagus Nerve Stimulation)

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drug-Resistant Epilepsy | **B** | 12-47% seizure reduction; 12-16% seizure-free at 24 weeks | Significant vs sham |
| Major Depression | **C** | Significant HAM-D reduction vs sham at 4 weeks; 27% response rate | Moderate |
| Anxiety (comorbid) | **C** | Improvement in anxiety scores alongside depression reduction | Moderate |
| Inflammation | **C** | Modulates functional brain networks; anti-inflammatory potential | Emerging |

### Session Parameters

| Parameter | Epilepsy | Depression |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 25 Hz (standard) or 1 Hz | 25 Hz |
| Pulse width | 200-300 microseconds | 200-300 microseconds |
| Intensity | Tolerance-adjusted (sub-motor threshold) | 0.5-4 mA |
| Duration | 20-60 min/session | 30-60 min/session |
| Sessions | Daily, ongoing | 12 weeks+ |
| Target | Cymba concha/tragus | Cymba concha |

### Response Rates
- Epilepsy: 12% seizure-free at 8 weeks, 16% at 24 weeks; 38-48% had seizure reduction
- Depression: HAM-D significantly reduced at week 4 vs sham; remission rate improved across 12 weeks (continuous improvement)

### Key Findings
- taVNS modifies brain network topology toward more integrated, less segregated networks
- Stabilizing effects on epileptic brain networks persist in up to 50% of subjects
- Antidepressant effects may work via NTS projections to limbic regions

### Key References
- PMC8042037 (taVNS epilepsy network effects)
- PMC4828906 (taVNS depression pilot study)
- PMC11543756 (VNS recent advances review)

## 6. TPS (Transcranial Pulse Stimulation)

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alzheimer's Disease (AD) | **B** | Younger subsample (<=70y): CERAD CTS +3.91 pts vs sham decline; BDI-II reduction | Significant for younger patients |
| Mild Cognitive Impairment | **C** | Single RCT (n=19); no adverse events | Preliminary |
| Major Depression | **C** | Pilot RCT (n=30); 6 sessions over 2 weeks to dlPFC | Positive trend |
| Parkinson's Disease | **C** | Motor cortex targeting; 65% had mild side effects | Exploratory |
| ADHD (pediatric) | **C** | RCT (n=32); left dlPFC targeting; 91% no AEs | Emerging |
| Autism Spectrum | **C** | RCT (n=32); rTPJ targeting; 84% no AEs | Emerging |

### Session Parameters

| Parameter | AD/MCI | Depression | PD |
|---|---|---|---|
| Energy flux density | 0.20-0.25 mJ/mm2 | 0.20-0.25 mJ/mm2 | 0.20-0.25 mJ/mm2 |
| Pulse repetition frequency | 4-5 Hz | 3-4 Hz | 4 Hz |
| Pulses/session | 6000 | 300 | 1500-4000 |
| Sessions | 6 over 2 weeks | 6 over 2 weeks | 1-10 |
| Target | Bilateral PFC, parietal, precuneus | dlPFC | S1, SMA, CMA |
| Duration | 2 weeks | 2 weeks | 2 weeks |

### Key Findings
- TPS is FDA-breakthrough designated and CE-marked for AD in EU
- Age-sensitive: effects significant only in <=70 years subgroup
- Increases memory-related brain activation in precuneus and attention network connectivity
- Significant BDI-II reduction at 3 months in per-protocol sample
- Safe: no major neuropathologic changes; mild AEs (depression, headache, dizziness)

### Key References
- JAMA Network Open 2025 (Matt et al. AD RCT, NCT03770182)
- PMC12655725 (TPS systematic review and meta-analysis, 2024)
- Brain Stimulation 2024 (TPS neuropsychiatric symptoms in AD)
- NCT05551585 (TPS MDD ongoing trial)

## 7. PBM/LLLT (Photobiomodulation/Low-Level Laser Therapy)

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traumatic Brain Injury (animal) | **C** | 1064nm LED: improved anxiety, depression-like behaviors, spatial cognition; neuroprotective | Preclinical |
| TBI (human) | **D** | Case series and pilot studies; improved cognitive function | Emerging |
| Depression | **D** | Limited human trials; transcranial NIR light proposed | Pilot |
| Cognitive Function | **C** | Enhances neurogenesis, synaptic remodeling; reduces neuroinflammation | Preclinical |

### Session Parameters (Preliminary)

| Parameter | TBI/Neuroprotection | Cognitive Enhancement |
|---|---|---|
| Wavelength | 1064 nm (NIR), 810 nm, 633 nm | 800-1100 nm |
| Power density | 25 mW/cm2 | 10-100 mW/cm2 |
| Duration | 12 min/day | 10-30 min |
| Sessions | 14 consecutive days | Ongoing research |
| Target | Frontal, parietal, global | Prefrontal |

### Key Findings
- 1064nm LED demonstrated multifaceted neuroprotective effects:
  - Inhibited neuronal apoptosis
  - Reduced brain injury volume
  - Promoted neurogenesis and synaptic remodeling
  - Upregulated neurotrophic factors (BDNF)
  - Enhanced blood-brain barrier integrity
  - Shifted microglia/astrocytes toward anti-inflammatory phenotypes
- Behavioral improvements: enhanced rotarod, open field exploration, Y-maze alternation

### Key References
- PMC12595661 (1064nm LED TBI study, Journal of Translational Medicine 2025)

## 8. Neurofeedback

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| ADHD | **A** | No meaningful benefit on probably blinded symptoms; small effects with standard protocols | SMD=0.04 (blinded); SMD=0.21 (standard protocols) |
| PTSD | **A** | EEG-NF shows large effects vs passive controls only; sham-controlled studies show no effect | Large (passive); null (sham-controlled) |
| Anxiety | **C** | Limited high-quality RCT evidence | Emerging |
| Cognitive Enhancement | **C** | Small improvement in processing speed only (SMD=0.35) | Minimal |

### Session Parameters

| Parameter | ADHD | PTSD |
|---|---|---|
| Modality | EEG-NF (theta/beta, SCP) | EEG-NF, fMRI-NF |
| Sessions | 30-40 | 10-30 |
| Frequency | 2-3x/week | 1-2x/week |
| Duration | 30-45 min | 20-40 min |
| Protocol | Theta/beta training, SMR, SCP | Alpha/theta, amygdala downregulation |

### Key Findings
- **ADHD:** 38 RCTs (n=2472). Probably blinded reports show NO significant improvement (SMD=0.04). Small significant effect only when restricted to standard protocols (SMD=0.21). Processing speed improved (SMD=0.35). Not recommended as stand-alone treatment.
- **PTSD:** Effects driven entirely by non-placebo-controlled studies. Sham-controlled RCTs show no significant effect. Dose-response observed with longer exposure.
- Standard protocols (theta/beta, slow cortical potential) show better results than novel approaches
- fMRI-NF and fNIRS-NF remain investigational

### Key References
- JAMA Psychiatry 2024 (Neurofeedback ADHD meta-analysis, 38 RCTs)
- PMID: 39661381 (Neurofeedback ADHD, 2025)
- PMC12708586 (Neurofeedback PTSD systematic review)
- VA/DoD Clinical Practice Guideline 2023: Insufficient evidence to recommend for or against NF for PTSD

## 9. Medication Changes

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| MDD - Antidepressants | **A** | Effect sizes 1.15-1.55 vs placebo (Cipriani network meta-analysis) | First-line treatment |
| MDD - Augmentation (antipsychotic) | **A** | Aripiprazole, quetiapine, brexpiprazule: significant augmentation effects | For partial responders |
| TRD - Combination strategies | **B** | Continue antidepressants during TMS; avoid bupropion/clomipramine (seizure risk) | See TMS + meds section |
| Mood Stabilizers (Bipolar) | **A** | Lithium, valproate, lamotrigine: established efficacy | Standard of care |
| OCD - SSRIs/SNRIs | **A** | Higher doses often needed; sertraline, fluoxetine, escitalopram first-line | Combined with ERP |

### Combination with Neuromodulation
- **TMS + Antidepressants:** Continue current antidepressants when initiating TMS to avoid need for re-initiation after TMS course
- **Avoid during TMS:** Bupropion, imipramine (lower seizure threshold)
- **TMS + Bupropion:** Paradoxically associated with INCREASED response in some studies
- **Maintenance:** Combining maintenance TMS with antidepressants shows higher sustained remission than either alone

### Key References
- PMC12044610 (TMS + antidepressants systematic review and meta-analysis)
- Cipriani et al. 2018 (Lancet network meta-analysis antidepressants)
- APA Practice Guidelines for MDD

## 10. Psychotherapy (CBT, DBT, ACT, EMDR)

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Therapy | Evidence Grade | Effect Size | Response Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Major Depression | CBT | **A** | Large (SMD -0.73 to -1.30 vs waitlist) | 40-60% |
| Depression (acute) | All psychotherapies | **A** | Comparable to antidepressants | Similar |
| PTSD | EMDR | **A** | Large (eta2=0.615 for trauma symptoms) | Effective vs CBT |
| PTSD | CBT / trauma-focused CBT | **A** | Large effect; first-line | 50-70% |
| Borderline PD | DBT | **A** | Large effect on suicidal behavior; moderate on core symptoms | Standard of care |
| Anxiety disorders | CBT, ACT | **A** | Large (SMD -0.89 to -1.35) | 60-80% |
| OCD | CBT (ERP) | **A** | Large; combined with SSRIs superior to either alone | 50-70% |
| Insomnia | CBT-I (see Section 15) | **A** | Large; first-line | 70-80% |

### Outcome Measures
- Depression: HAM-D, BDI-II, PHQ-9, MADRS
- PTSD: CAPS-5, PCL-5, ITQ (International Trauma Questionnaire)
- Anxiety: GAD-7, HAM-A, SAS/SDS
- BPD: PAI-BOR, number of suicide attempts, hospitalizations
- Post-traumatic growth: PTGI (Post-Traumatic Growth Inventory)

### Key Findings
- EMDR and CBT show equivalent outcomes for PTSD; EMDR may have processing speed benefits
- DBT: Established standard for BPD; reduces suicidal behavior, hospitalizations
- ACT: Effective for anxiety, depression; focuses on psychological flexibility
- Combined psychotherapy + pharmacotherapy: Superior to either alone for most conditions

### Key References
- PMC12059781 (EMDR vs CBT for BPD/trauma RCT)
- APA Clinical Practice Guidelines
- NICE Guidelines NG222 (Depression)

## 13. Physiotherapy

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Evidence Grade | Key Finding | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parkinson's Disease - Motor | **A** | Improves gait, balance, strength, UPDRS scores; specialized PT reduces complications | SMD -0.53 to -0.89 |
| Parkinson's Disease - Fall Prevention | **A** | Balance training, LSVT-BIG, amplitude-based training effective | Moderate-large |
| Chronic Pain | **A** | Exercise therapy core component; improves function and reduces pain | Moderate |
| Functional Movement Disorder | **B** | Specialist physiotherapy effective (Physio4FMD RCT, Lancet Neurology 2024) | Significant |
| Stroke Rehabilitation | **A** | Task-specific training, gait re-education, early mobilization | Moderate-large |

### Core PT Domains for Parkinson's Disease
1. Physical performance and pain
2. Transfers (sit-to-stand, bed mobility)
3. Hand and arm use
4. Balance (proprioceptive training)
5. Gait (amplitude-based: LSVT-BIG)
6. Cardiopulmonary endurance
7. Home-based exercise programs

### Exercise as Medicine in PD
- High-intensity aerobic exercise (80-85% max HR) more effective than moderate (60-65%)
- 3-4 days/week for 6 months shows potential disease-modifying effects
- Progressive resistance exercise over 2 years: clinically relevant UPDRS-III reduction
- May stabilize corticostriatal sensorimotor network and reduce global brain atrophy

### Key References
- JNNP 2024 (Exercise as medicine in PD)
- Cochrane 2024: Physical exercise for PD (CD013856)
- Lancet Neurology 2024 (Physio4FMD RCT)
- PMC13097574 (PD rehabilitation review)

## 15. Sleep Interventions

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Intervention | Evidence Grade | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chronic Insomnia | CBT-I (in-person) | **A** | Large (SMD -1.30); first-line treatment | 
| Chronic Insomnia | Digital CBT-I | **A** | Large (SMD -1.01 to -1.35) |
| Chronic Insomnia | Group CBT-I | **A** | Large (SMD -1.01); resource-saving alternative |
| Chronic Insomnia | Telehealth CBT-I | **A** | Large (SMD -1.35); highest ranked in network meta-analysis |
| Insomnia + Comorbidities | Combination (dCBT-I + meds) | **A** | Superior to monotherapy |

### CBT-I Components (Effectiveness)
1. **Relaxation Training:** Most completed (98.4%)
2. **Sleep Restriction:** 92.07% completion
3. **Stimulus Control:** 48.68% completion
4. **Cognitive Reconstruction:** 44.33% completion
5. **Sleep Hygiene:** 56% initial engagement

### Outcome Measures
- Primary: ISI, PSQI, SCI
- Secondary: ESS (daytime somnolence), sleep diary parameters
- Objective: Actigraphy, polysomnography (effects smaller on objective measures)

### Key Findings
- All CBT-I delivery formats (in-person, digital, group, telehealth, bibliotherapy) significantly effective
- No single format consistently superior across all outcomes
- Telehealth and face-to-face ranked highest for insomnia severity
- Effects more substantial on subjective vs objective sleep measures

## 17. Exercise Interventions

### Key Indications with Evidence Strength

| Indication | Exercise Type | Evidence Grade | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Depression (older adults) | Mind-body (yoga, tai chi) | **A** | SMD=-0.89 (depression); SMD=-0.77 (anxiety) |

| Parkinson's Disease | Aerobic 80-85% HR max | **A** | Potential disease-modifying effects |
| Chronic Pain | Multimodal exercise | **A** | Moderate effect on pain and function |

### Dose-Response Evidence
- **Minimum threshold:** ~176 min/week moderate-intensity exercise for cognitive benefits
- **Moderate intensity** produces largest cognitive gains (g=0.36)
- **Inpatient** settings show larger effects than outpatient
- **Combined** with other therapies shows larger effects than exercise alone
- **Domain-specific:** Executive function, attention, and memory improve; verbal fluency and processing speed less responsive

### Exercise Types Ranked
1. **Mind-body (tai chi, yoga, qigong):** Largest effects for depression/anxiety in older adults
2. **Aerobic:** Good for depression, cardiovascular health
3. **Resistance training:** Good for strength, BDNF, disease modification in PD
4. **Multicomponent:** Best overall outcomes when combined

## 21. Measurement-Based Care (MBC) Frameworks

### Core Principles

**Three Guiding Principles (APA Guidelines):**
1. **Standardisation:** Consistent use of validated measures
2. **Transparency:** Open sharing of results with patients
3. **Simplification:** Technology integration to reduce burden

### Recommended Measurement Framework

| Domain | Screening | Baseline | During Treatment | Endpoint |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Depression severity | PHQ-9 | HAM-D or MADRS | PHQ-9 (every 1-2 weeks) | HAM-D/MADRS + PHQ-9 |
| Anxiety severity | GAD-7 | HAM-A | GAD-7 (every 1-2 weeks) | GAD-7 + HAM-A |
| Suicide risk | Columbia/C-SSRS | C-SSRS | At every session initially | C-SSRS |
| Cognitive function | MoCA or ACE-III | Full battery | MoCA (every 4 weeks) | Full battery |
| Sleep | ISI or PSQI | Polysomnography | ISI (every 2 weeks) | ISI + actigraphy |
| Quality of life | EQ-5D or SF-36 | Full assessment | EQ-5D (every 4 weeks) | Full assessment |
| Functioning | WHODAS 2.0 | Full assessment | WHODAS (every 4 weeks) | Full assessment |
| Side effects | Self-report checklist | Physical exam | At every session | Full assessment |

### Technology-Enhanced MBC
- **Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT):** Reduces question redundancy by ~50%
- **Electronic health record integration:** Automated scoring and tracking
- **Real-time symptom monitoring:** Mobile app-based reporting
- **Dashboard visualization:** Trend analysis for shared decision-making

- PMC12406929 (MBC systematic review patient/provider perspectives)
- APA Professional Practice Guidelines on Measurement-Based Care (2024)
